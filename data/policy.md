# Small Business Loan Policies and Procedures
Version 3.2 | Effective: January 2026

## Section 1: Eligibility Requirements

To qualify for a standard small business loan, applicants must meet the following baseline criteria:

- Minimum personal credit score of 650 for loans up to $50,000
- Minimum personal credit score of 700 for loans between $50,001 and $250,000
- Minimum personal credit score of 740 for loans above $250,000
- Business must be operational for at least 24 months
- Business must be registered as an LLC, S-Corp, C-Corp, or Sole Proprietorship
- Owner must hold at least 20% equity stake in the business
- Applicant must be a US citizen or permanent resident
- Business must be physically located within the United States

Startup loans (for businesses operational less than 24 months) are available up to $25,000 with credit score 720+ and a comprehensive business plan.

## Section 2: Financial Requirements

Annual revenue thresholds based on loan amount:
- Loans up to $50,000: minimum $75,000 annual revenue
- Loans $50,001-$150,000: minimum $200,000 annual revenue
- Loans $150,001-$500,000: minimum $750,000 annual revenue
- Loans above $500,000: minimum $2,000,000 annual revenue

Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio policies:
- DTI below 36%: standard approval consideration
- DTI between 36-43%: approval with possible conditions
- DTI between 43-50%: requires manual underwriter review
- DTI above 50%: automatic decline

The business must demonstrate positive cash flow for at least 18 of the last 24 months. Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) must exceed 1.25 for approval.

## Section 3: Loan Amounts, Terms, and Interest Rates

Maximum loan amounts:
- First-time borrowers: capped at $100,000
- Established borrowers (2+ successful repayment history): up to $500,000
- Premium tier borrowers (5+ year relationship, excellent record): up to $2,000,000

Standard loan terms:
- Working capital loans: 1-3 years
- Equipment financing: 3-7 years
- Real estate-backed loans: 5-15 years
- SBA-guaranteed loans: up to 25 years

Interest rate ranges:
- Tier 1 (credit 740+, low DTI): 6.5% - 9.0% APR
- Tier 2 (credit 700-739): 9.0% - 12.5% APR
- Tier 3 (credit 650-699): 12.5% - 16.0% APR
- Variable rate loans available with rates tied to Prime + margin

## Section 4: Required Documentation

All applicants must provide:
- Last 2 years of business tax returns (Forms 1120, 1120S, 1065, or Schedule C)
- Last 2 years of personal tax returns for owners with 20%+ stake
- Last 6 months of business bank statements
- Year-to-date profit and loss statement
- Year-to-date balance sheet
- Business license and registration documents
- Government-issued photo ID for all owners

Additional documentation for loans above $250,000:
- Comprehensive business plan with 3-year projections
- Accounts receivable and accounts payable aging reports
- Personal financial statement (PFS) for all owners
- Resumes of key management personnel

For real estate-secured loans:
- Property appraisal (within last 6 months)
- Property insurance documentation
- Title report

## Section 5: Industry Restrictions and High-Risk Categories

Prohibited industries (no loans extended):
- Cannabis cultivation, distribution, or retail
- Gambling, gaming, or sports betting operations
- Adult entertainment businesses
- Firearms manufacturing or wholesale distribution
- Speculative real estate ventures
- Multi-level marketing (MLM) businesses
- Cryptocurrency trading or mining operations
- Payday lending or check cashing services

High-risk industries (require enhanced underwriting):
- Restaurants and food service: 30% higher revenue threshold
- Retail (non-essential): 25% higher revenue threshold
- Construction and contracting: 12 months of contracts in pipeline
- Trucking and logistics: 18 months minimum operational history
- Hospitality and tourism: seasonal revenue analysis required
- Beauty and personal care services: location-based viability assessment

## Section 6: Approval Decisions and Conditions

Decision categories:
- Approved: meets all criteria, standard terms apply
- Approved with Conditions: requires additional documentation, collateral, or guarantees before funding
- Pending Review: requires manual underwriter assessment, typical turnaround 5-7 business days
- Declined: does not meet minimum criteria

Common approval conditions:
- Personal guarantee from all owners with 20%+ stake
- Additional collateral requirement (equipment, real estate, accounts receivable)
- Higher interest rate (typically 1-3% above standard tier)
- Reduced loan amount from requested
- Co-signer or guarantor requirement
- Mandatory business insurance maintenance
- Quarterly financial reporting requirement

Conditional approvals expire after 30 days if conditions are not met. Applicants may resubmit after 6 months with material improvements to their financial profile.

## Section 7: Collateral and Security Requirements

Loans above $100,000 typically require collateral:
- Business equipment and machinery
- Commercial real estate
- Accounts receivable (factoring agreements)
- Inventory (with regular monitoring)
- Personal real estate (with spousal consent if applicable)

Loan-to-Value (LTV) limits by collateral type:
- Commercial real estate: up to 80% LTV
- Equipment (new): up to 90% LTV
- Equipment (used, less than 5 years): up to 75% LTV
- Accounts receivable: up to 80% of qualified receivables
- Inventory: up to 50% of cost or market value

UCC-1 financing statements will be filed for all secured loans. Property insurance naming the lender as loss payee is required throughout the loan term.

## Section 8: Special Programs and Considerations

SBA-guaranteed loan programs:
- SBA 7(a): up to $5 million, working capital and general purposes
- SBA 504: real estate and equipment, up to $5.5 million
- SBA Express: expedited processing up to $500,000
- Microloans: up to $50,000 for startups and small needs

Veteran-owned business benefits:
- Reduced fees on SBA-guaranteed loans
- Priority processing (3-day turnaround for complete applications)
- Dedicated underwriting team

Women-owned and minority-owned business programs:
- Reduced documentation requirements for loans under $50,000
- Mentorship program enrollment for first-time borrowers
- Lower interest rate margins (0.25-0.50% reduction)

Disaster recovery loans:
- Available to businesses in federally declared disaster areas
- Deferred payments for first 6 months
- Reduced interest rates (typically 4-6%)
